Guilty Gear (1998)

The original Guilty Gear was released for PlayStation, introducing the world to Sol Badguy, Ky Kiske, and the post-apocalyptic world of Gears. The game stood out with its heavy metal aesthetic and unique character designs by Daisuke Ishiwatari.

Platform: PlayStation
Characters: 10 fighters

Guilty Gear X (2000)

Guilty Gear X transitioned the series to gorgeous high-resolution 2D sprites that would become the series' signature style. The game expanded the roster significantly and refined the gameplay mechanics.

Platform: Arcade, Dreamcast, PS2
Characters: 16 fighters

Guilty Gear XX (2002)

Often considered the definitive classic Guilty Gear, XX (The Midnight Carnival) refined the gameplay to near perfection. The game received numerous updated versions including #Reload, Slash, Accent Core, and Accent Core Plus R.

Platform: Arcade, PS2, Xbox
Characters: 20+ fighters

Guilty Gear 2: Overture (2007)

A significant departure from the fighting game formula, Overture was a hybrid action game with real-time strategy elements. While divisive among fans, it expanded the series' lore significantly.

Platform: Xbox 360, PC
Genre: Action RTS hybrid

Guilty Gear Xrd (2014)

Xrd revolutionized the series with 3D models designed to look like 2D anime sprites. Using the Unreal Engine, the game maintained the series' visual flair while modernizing the presentation. Xrd received updates with -SIGN-, -REVELATOR-, and REV 2 versions.

Platform: Arcade, PS3, PS4, PC
Visual Style: 2.5D "anime" aesthetic

Guilty Gear Strive (2021)

Strive represents a bold new direction for the series, with redesigned mechanics focused on accessibility while maintaining depth. The game features stunning visuals, rollback netcode for superior online play, and a continued focus on rock-solid gameplay.

Platform: PS4, PS5, PC, Xbox
Innovation: Rollback netcode

Guilty Gear Strive Season 2 (2023)

Season 2 expanded Strive with additional characters, balance updates, and new gameplay mechanics. The DLC added fan-favorites like Bridget and Sin Kiske alongside newcomers like Bedman? and Asuka R#.

Additions: 4 new characters
Features: Crossplay between all platforms
